India's Highest Mountains

Explore the most magnificent peaks in the Indian subcontinent

Kanchenjunga

8,586m

Location: Sikkim-Nepal Border

First Ascent: 1955

Difficulty: Extreme

Best Season: April-May, September-October

The third highest mountain in the world, Kanchenjunga is revered as sacred by the local people. Its name means "The Five Treasures of Snows" representing the five peaks of the mountain.

Sacred Peak Technical Climb Permit Required

Nanda Devi

7,816m

Location: Uttarakhand

First Ascent: 1936

Difficulty: Very High

Best Season: May-June, September

India's second highest peak and the highest mountain entirely within India. Part of the Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve, it's considered one of the most beautiful mountains in the world.

Biosphere Reserve UNESCO Site Restricted Access

Kamet

7,756m

Location: Uttarakhand

First Ascent: 1931

Difficulty: High

Best Season: May-June, September-October

The second highest summit in the Garhwal region, Kamet was the first peak over 7,500m to be climbed. It offers spectacular views of the surrounding Himalayan peaks.

Historic Peak Accessible Great Views

Saltoro Kangri

7,742m

Location: Karakoram Range

First Ascent: 1962

Difficulty: Very High

Best Season: June-August

Located in the Karakoram range, this peak offers challenging climbing conditions with stunning glacier views and is part of the disputed territory region.

Karakoram Glaciated Remote

Saser Kangri

7,672m

Location: Ladakh, Karakoram

First Ascent: 1973

Difficulty: Very High

Best Season: June-September

The highest peak in the Saser Muztagh subrange of the Karakoram, known for its technical climbing routes and pristine wilderness setting.

Technical Wilderness High Altitude

Mamostong Kangri

7,516m

Location: Ladakh, Karakoram

First Ascent: 1984

Difficulty: High

Best Season: June-August

Located in the remote Rimo Muztagh of the Karakoram, this peak offers challenging high-altitude mountaineering in one of the world's most remote locations.

Remote Challenging Pristine
